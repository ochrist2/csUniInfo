Why York (The Computer Science Program)?

U of T has the most prestigious CS program in Canada, and it mainly has to do with the reputation of the school as a whole coupled with the style of teaching. Click on the header to read what U of T has to say about their program. However, to give a run down, U of T teaches CS differently than most universities by getting the students to practice programming while in the lecture. I find this to be the most effective tool in learning, as practice makes perfect, especially when it comes to the more difficult aspects of programming. Along with a different style of teaching, U of T has more teaching professors in the CS department than any other university in Ontario.

Why York (The School)?

The University of Toronto is the highest rated university in Ontario, and is regularly the top university in Canada. Being the 32nd best university in the world in 2016 comes from the universities extensive research, academic reputation, and reputation with employers. The main reasons why you'd want to go to U of T for your undergraduate degree comes from the prestige of the university, which will allow you to hopefully join one of the city of Toronto's many tech companies ranging from a small start up to tech giants like Apple. Between your third and fourth year you have the opportunity to do an industry internship for 8, 12 or 16 months where you will be able to gain work experience before you graduate, and given that the school is in Toronto, going to U of T gives you an exceptional leg up on getting a job in the city. Another great thing about U of T is its large number of teaching professors. Although U of T's reputation, and incredible academics are great reasons to attend the school, the work load is very difficult, and the "student experience" is rated rather low. Due to the difficulty, and lower focus on the student life at U of T, many students find themselves overwhelmed with work, and rather lonely due to the lack of a definite community. This is not to say that if you go to U of T you'll have a bad experience, but it is important to keep in mind that many people have brought these complaints to light, and there is some truth behind them. Overall Toronto is the best school in Ontario and the entirety of Canada. If you're looking for the best education in computer science, pursue the University of Toronto.

How do I get in?

U of T do not have the exact average for admissions posted on their site, however Electronicinfo.ca says that the grade range for acceptance is from the low 90's. From personal experience, I find this to be true. If you have above a 90% average from a well regarded highschool (universities keep track of the highschools which have inflated grades), you have a decent chance of admission, but the only the way to be sure of acceptance is to have around a 93-95% average. Your most important classes when applying to CS at U of T are Calculus and English. Since these are the only required courses for the program, they will be regarded more than other classes.

First year module:

  • CSC108H1: Introduction to Computer Programming[36L] (if you have prior programming experience this can be skipped)
  • CSC148H1: Introduction to Computer Science[36L/24P]
  • CSC165H1: Mathematical Expression and Reasoning for Computer Science[36L/24T]
  • MAT135H1: Calculus 1(A)[36L/12T]
  • MAT135H1: Calculus 1(B )[36L/12T]

It is important to note that you are required to get a minimum of a 60% in all these classes to continue as a CS major. Information about later years can be found on the module linked above.

Info About Electives:

You have 2.5 credits, or 5 half year courses to choose as electives for your first year at U of T. You can take almost anything as your elective, and it is encouraged by the Arts and Science department to branch out and try things you normally wouldn't, or something you have a passion for, but don't want to major in.

First Year Language:

CSC108H1 is taught in Python, but as you progress with the degree you may touch on a wide variety of languages

Department:

CS at U of T is under the department of Arts and Science. This does not mean much considering most programs at U of T lie in this department.
